Getting Started with Our Service

Step 1: IAM

Go to your Amazon Web Services Console Home. Once there, navigate to IAM/Search up IAM.

Step 2: Finding Roles

In IAM, click on Roles under Access Management.

Step 3: Setting up a New Role

Click on the Create Role button.

Step 2: Trusted Entity Type

Select AWS account as Trusted Entity Type.

Step 5: Filling out the Trusted Entity Type

Select 'Another AWS Account' and type in 536394966329.

Step 6

Once those 2 steps are completed, your page should look like this. Press Next.

Step 7: Setting up Permissions

Select the 'AmazonS3FullAccess' permission box and press Next.

Step 8: Finishing up the Custom Role Setup

Create a meaningful name for the role and make sure you remember it/write it down somewhere. Note: The role name you create is case-sensitive. Make sure the role you enter in this field is the same role name you used to register with.

Step 9: Finishing up the Custom Role Setup

Make sure that the add permission section and the tag section look like this. You don't need to add any tags.

Step 10: Log Back In

After you have completed the previous steps, you can head on over and register with our service. As seen in the example, fill out the fields with your own values. Assuming you named your role from Step 8 as 'awsRole' as seen in the example, it is critical that you write the same name down. Note: Our application and AWS is case-sensitive so it's important that you write down your role name as you see it
